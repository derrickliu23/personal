Research

I'm working toward a PhD at the intersection of computational biology and computer science — specifically in how chromatin architecture and histone variants regulate gene expression. I'm drawn to questions where single-molecule imaging and quantitative modeling meet molecular biology.

Histone Variant Biology & Gene Regulation

Focused on H2A.Z and its role in nucleosome dynamics at gene promoters. Interested in how variant incorporation shapes accessibility and transcriptional output at a mechanistic level.

Single-Molecule Imaging

Drawn to live-cell fluorescence and super-resolution approaches that let you watch individual transcription factors and chromatin regulators in action — rather than inferring behavior from population averages.

Computational Genomics

Applying sequence analysis, motif discovery, and ML models to understand cis-regulatory elements. Actively building tools (like Promoter Analyzer) to practice these methods hands-on.
